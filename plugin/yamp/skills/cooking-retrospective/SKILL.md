> **Prerequisite** — if you haven't already this session, read the `yamp-core` skill before continuing.

# Retrospective

Call `retrospective(period, spend_range?, waste_range?, waste_mapping_version?)` and summarize the patterns that matter: protein/cuisine mix (real cook counts, not recency), cadence (cooks/week — `recipe` + `ad_hoc` only — with the per-meal split from `cadence.by_meal`: lead with the meals I actually plan, e.g. "about 4 dinners and 2 lunches a week"; `meal_unknown` counts older meal-less entries, so don't read it as a gap), the cook-vs-convenience split, ready-to-eat favorites, and **underused** — loved recipes (my favorites, or ones I cook a lot) that have gone quiet and are in season now. Frame a revival off each item's `why`: a `favorite` is "you starred X but haven't made it lately" (or "…and never have" when `last_cooked` is null); a `revealed` one is "you used to make X all the time" — lean on `cook_count` there. If `underused_count` exceeds the list shown, say there are more. Tie it to my diet principles when relevant ("you're light on fish this month vs. your once-a-week target"). Surface patterns; don't nag — one or two revival nudges at most.

For a Spend question, pass the requested `spend_range` (`4w`, `8w`, or `12w`); when no Spend range is requested, keep the compatible `4w` default rather than implying a different window. State the actual UTC date bounds from the result. This is captured household grocery spend, not a bank or final-receipt ledger: it is a read-only analysis of non-voided spend events, and items in `awaiting_mark_placed` are a separate notice that contributes to no amount.

Lead with coverage before conclusions. `empty` means no recorded purchase events in the window. `unavailable` means events exist but no usable price — report the event/unpriced counts and do not call the zero known subtotal “$0 spend.” `partial` amounts are **known subtotals**: name the unpriced, estimated, or pending-department counts the result gives you, and never invent a remainder, silently fill a missing value, or reclassify a captured department/store/provenance. With `complete`, the reported captured values can be stated directly. Use the server's deterministic `insight` as a concise evidence-backed lead, not as permission to add another guessed insight.

Explain ratios by their actual denominators when useful. Average per week always divides by all selected ISO-week buckets, including empty/current-partial buckets. Cost per meal counts each logged `recipe` or `ad_hoc` cook once and excludes capture-stamped Household and Beverages spend from its numerator; it is not per serving. If its reason is `zero_meals` or `numerator_unavailable`, say that rather than computing your own ratio. Report trend only when its status is `available`; for `current_incomplete`, `prior_incomplete`, or `prior_zero`, explain why comparison is unavailable and do not substitute a percent. A budget comparison is over only when `over_budget` is true, within only when false, and unknown when null; a null `weekly_budget` supports no budget claim. Breakdowns and top drivers already carry their correct captured denominators and deterministic ordering — relay them without recomputing or treating an absent group as evidence of zero activity.

For a Waste question, pass the requested `waste_range` (`4w`, `8w`, or `12w`); when none is requested, keep the compatible `4w` default. Pass `waste_mapping_version` only when I explicitly ask to replay a supported named policy; otherwise use current. State the returned UTC date bounds and mapping version. Treat `.waste` as the only Waste-analysis authority: relay its returned counts, values, coverage, reasons, ordering, and exact `insight` without recalculating, reclassifying, or combining it with pantry guesses, item-name lore, recipes, package quantities, another household, or outside prices.

Lead Waste with monetary coverage. `empty` means no recorded tosses and exact zero Items binned. `unavailable` means tosses exist but none has a matching eligible last-paid price — say **Last-paid value unavailable**, give the returned event/unmatched counts, and never call known amount zero "$0 waste." `partial` is a **Known last-paid estimate**: name the returned unmatched and estimated counts and never invent the missing remainder. `complete` is still a **Last-paid estimate**, not a receipt total, member-entered value, or measured tossed-quantity value. A pending Waste department is separate classification evidence and never turns complete Waste money into partial money; say what is classified/pending from `coverage.department`.

Use exact returned denominators. Items binned counts persisted toss rows and items per week uses every selected bucket. Waste-derived dollars are spend-history last-paid estimates; `qualifying_spend_amount` is recorded/captured grocery Spend, not another per-toss estimate. Report Waste rate only when available; otherwise give its returned `waste_incomplete`, `spend_incomplete`, or `zero_denominator` reason and qualifying-Spend coverage. Report Waste trend only when available; otherwise give `current_incomplete`, `prior_incomplete`, or `prior_zero` plus the returned current/prior known amounts — there are no prior coverage counts to infer. Department uses only effectively classified denominators; Reason and Avoidability use all-event denominators. Breakdowns, most-wasted groups, and weekly rows are already deterministically ordered and coverage-aware, so do not reorder them or treat an absent/unvalued group as zero.

The Waste `insight` is server-authored evidence, not an invitation to improvise a diagnosis or prevention tip. Quote or paraphrase only what its returned facts support. Do not invent item-specific advice, infer avoidability beyond the returned mapping, edit a Waste event, write a value/classification, invoke a fill, or change my profile because of a Waste result. This analyzer flow is read-only and has no direct event, derived-value, avoidability, aggregate, edit, delete, or correction writer. The separate existing `update_pantry` flow may capture a qualitative waste disposition but accepts no dollar value; do not invoke it merely because an analysis was read. If I ask what to do next, do not manufacture Waste-prevention advice from the aggregate. You may offer a handoff to an existing planning, pantry, guidance, or profile flow, but describe that as a separate action and wait for my approval before invoking it.

**Offer to fold a real pattern back into my profile.** When the history reveals something durable that my taste profile or diet principles don't already capture — a cuisine I clearly gravitate to, a protein I keep skipping, a variety target reality says I should adjust ("you've set fish weekly but average twice a month — want to make that the target, or should I push fish harder?") — *offer* to update it: `update_taste` for a taste lean, `update_diet_principles` for a target or rule. Same posture as everywhere else: **suggest, never write on your own** — propose the specific edit, and only call the tool once I say yes. One or two offers at most; don't turn a summary into an interrogation.
