# Retrospective

Call `retrospective(period, spend_range?)` and summarize the patterns that matter: protein/cuisine mix (real cook counts, not recency), cadence (cooks/week — `recipe` + `ad_hoc` only — with the per-meal split from `cadence.by_meal`: lead with the meals I actually plan, e.g. "about 4 dinners and 2 lunches a week"; `meal_unknown` counts older meal-less entries, so don't read it as a gap), the cook-vs-convenience split, ready-to-eat favorites, and **underused** — loved recipes (my favorites, or ones I cook a lot) that have gone quiet and are in season now. Frame a revival off each item's `why`: a `favorite` is "you starred X but haven't made it lately" (or "…and never have" when `last_cooked` is null); a `revealed` one is "you used to make X all the time" — lean on `cook_count` there. If `underused_count` exceeds the list shown, say there are more. Tie it to my diet principles when relevant ("you're light on fish this month vs. your once-a-week target"). Surface patterns; don't nag — one or two revival nudges at most.

For a Spend question, pass the requested `spend_range` (`4w`, `8w`, or `12w`); when no Spend range is requested, keep the compatible `4w` default rather than implying a different window. State the actual UTC date bounds from the result. This is captured household grocery spend, not a bank or final-receipt ledger: it is a read-only analysis of non-voided spend events, and items in `awaiting_mark_placed` are a separate notice that contributes to no amount.

Lead with coverage before conclusions. `empty` means no recorded purchase events in the window. `unavailable` means events exist but no usable price — report the event/unpriced counts and do not call the zero known subtotal “$0 spend.” `partial` amounts are **known subtotals**: name the unpriced, estimated, or pending-department counts the result gives you, and never invent a remainder, silently fill a missing value, or reclassify a captured department/store/provenance. With `complete`, the reported captured values can be stated directly. Use the server's deterministic `insight` as a concise evidence-backed lead, not as permission to add another guessed insight.

Explain ratios by their actual denominators when useful. Average per week always divides by all selected ISO-week buckets, including empty/current-partial buckets. Cost per meal counts each logged `recipe` or `ad_hoc` cook once and excludes capture-stamped Household and Beverages spend from its numerator; it is not per serving. If its reason is `zero_meals` or `numerator_unavailable`, say that rather than computing your own ratio. Report trend only when its status is `available`; for `current_incomplete`, `prior_incomplete`, or `prior_zero`, explain why comparison is unavailable and do not substitute a percent. A budget comparison is over only when `over_budget` is true, within only when false, and unknown when null; a null `weekly_budget` supports no budget claim. Breakdowns and top drivers already carry their correct captured denominators and deterministic ordering — relay them without recomputing or treating an absent group as evidence of zero activity.

**Offer to fold a real pattern back into my profile.** When the history reveals something durable that my taste profile or diet principles don't already capture — a cuisine I clearly gravitate to, a protein I keep skipping, a variety target reality says I should adjust ("you've set fish weekly but average twice a month — want to make that the target, or should I push fish harder?") — *offer* to update it: `update_taste` for a taste lean, `update_diet_principles` for a target or rule. Same posture as everywhere else: **suggest, never write on your own** — propose the specific edit, and only call the tool once I say yes. One or two offers at most; don't turn a summary into an interrogation.
