## Ingredients

- 2 blocks firm or extra firm tofu (14–16 oz each)
- ⅔ cup cornstarch
- ¼ cup un-toasted sesame seeds
- ¾ tsp fine sea salt
- ½ tsp white pepper
- ½ tsp garlic powder
- ½ tsp onion powder
- ¼ cup neutral oil (vegetable, canola, or avocado)

**Sauce:**
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- ¾ cup water, low-sodium vegetable stock, or low-sodium chicken stock
- 3 tbsp sugar (brown sugar also works)
- 1½ tbsp vegetarian oyster sauce (or regular oyster sauce)
- 1 tbsp light soy sauce
- 1 tbsp honey
- 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine or rice vinegar (sub: extra 1 tbsp rice vinegar for non-alcoholic)
- 1½ tsp rice vinegar
- 1½ tsp sesame oil
- ⅛ tsp white pepper
- 2 tsp cornstarch + 1 tbsp water (slurry)
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds + thinly sliced scallion (garnish)

## Instructions

1. Drain tofu blocks. Gently squeeze to release water or pat dry. Cut into 1-inch cubes.
2. In a shallow bowl, combine cornstarch, sesame seeds, salt, white pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder. Working in 2 batches, dredge tofu cubes in the mixture, pressing to cover all sides.
3. Heat a large nonstick pan or cast-iron skillet over medium-high. Add neutral oil. When shimmering, fry first batch of tofu 4–5 minutes per side, flipping to crisp all sides, until golden. Transfer to a plate. Repeat with second batch. Remove all tofu.
4. In the same skillet, add minced garlic and cook 1 minute. Add water/stock, sugar, oyster sauce, soy sauce, honey, Shaoxing wine, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and white pepper. Simmer 3 minutes.
5. Stir cornstarch slurry and add to sauce. Cook 1 more minute until thickened. Return tofu and toss to coat.
6. Serve over rice, garnished with toasted sesame seeds and scallion.
