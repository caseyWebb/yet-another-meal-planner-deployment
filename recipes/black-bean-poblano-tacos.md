## Ingredients

**Fresh corn salsa:**
- 2 ears corn, shucked (about 1½ to 2 cups kernels)
- 1 small red bell pepper, finely diced
- ¼ cup fresh cilantro leaves, chopped
- Juice of 1 lime
- Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper

**Black bean filling:**
- 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
- ½ large white or yellow onion, finely diced
- 1 poblano pepper, seeded, stemmed, and finely diced
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- ¾ teaspoon ground chili powder
- 2 (15-oz) cans black beans (undrained)
- 1 tablespoon fresh lime juice
- ½ to 1 teaspoon jalapeño hot sauce (optional)
- Small handful chopped cilantro
- Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper

**To serve:**
- 8 to 12 small soft corn tortillas
- Guacamole (store-bought or homemade)
- Crumbled cotija cheese
- Fresh cilantro
- Jalapeño hot sauce (optional)

## Instructions

1. **Corn salsa:** Combine the raw corn, bell pepper, cilantro, and lime juice in a bowl. Toss and season with salt and pepper. Set aside.
2. **Black beans:** Warm the olive oil in a saucepan over medium. Add the onion and poblano and sauté 5 to 7 minutes until softened and the onion is translucent. Add the cumin and chili powder and sauté 30 to 45 seconds. Add the undrained black beans, stir, and simmer uncovered over medium-low for 12 to 15 minutes until slightly thickened. Off the heat, stir in the lime juice, hot sauce, and cilantro. Season to taste and keep warm.
3. **Tortillas:** Char each corn tortilla directly over a medium gas flame, 15 to 25 seconds per side, flipping with tongs. Keep warm wrapped in a towel.
4. **Assemble:** Fill the tortillas with black beans (use a slotted spoon), then top with guacamole, corn salsa, cotija, and cilantro. Serve immediately.
