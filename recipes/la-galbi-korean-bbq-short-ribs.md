Korean BBQ short ribs marinated in a sweet-savory sauce with pear or apple as a natural tenderizer. Can be grilled, broiled, or pan-fried. Best marinated overnight.

## Ingredients

- 3–4 lbs cross-cut beef short ribs (LA galbi / flaken cut, ~⅓-inch thick)
- ½ Korean/Asian pear (or 1 apple), grated
- 2 Tbsp minced garlic (~6 cloves)
- ½ medium onion, grated
- 1 tsp minced ginger
- ½ cup soy sauce
- ½ cup water
- 3–4 Tbsp sugar
- 2 Tbsp honey
- ¼ cup cooking rice wine (mirin or Korean matsul mirim)
- 2 Tbsp sesame oil
- 3 scallions, finely chopped
- ½ tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp sesame seeds (optional)

## Instructions

1. Rinse ribs to remove bone dust. Drain well. Lightly pound with a meat tenderizer or stretch with your hands to tenderize.
2. Blend pear (or apple) with onion, garlic, and ginger as finely as possible. Combine with all remaining marinade ingredients in a large bowl or container.
3. Add meat and coat each rib. Marinate 6–8 hours, flipping halfway through (overnight for best results).
4. **Grill:** Preheat to medium-high. Grill 2–3 minutes per side, turning once.
5. **Broil:** Set oven to broil. Place ribs on broiler pan on top rack (~6 inches below broiler). Cook 5–6 minutes, flip, cook 3–4 more minutes. Watch closely.
6. **Pan-fry:** Preheat large non-stick pan. Reduce to medium. Cook in a single layer 4–5 minutes per side, adding strained marinade when pan dries. Cook until browned and sauce is gone.
