## Ingredients

**Black-eyed peas (to cook, or use 5 cups canned):**
- 1 lb dried black-eyed peas (or 3 × 14-oz cans)
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 6 cups water or stock
- 2 bay leaves
- 3 garlic cloves or 1 shallot

**Smoky beans:**
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 onion, diced
- 8 cloves garlic, rough chopped
- 5 cups cooked black-eyed peas
- 1 teaspoon salt, more to taste
- 1 to 2 teaspoons smoked paprika
- Pinch allspice (¼ teaspoon, more to taste)
- Pinch cayenne (optional)
- ½ to 1 cup broth or bean cooking liquid

**Garlicky collards:**
- 6 cloves garlic
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- ¼ teaspoon salt
- ¼ teaspoon chili flakes, more to taste
- 2 to 3 bunches collard greens (12 to 15 leaves)

**To finish:**
- A simple gremolata (finely chopped parsley + minced garlic + lemon zest, with a little olive oil and salt)
- Cornbread, for serving

## Instructions

1. **Cook the beans:** In an Instant Pot, pressure cook the beans, water, salt, bay leaves, and garlic on high 15 minutes; release after 10. Drain, saving the liquid. (Stovetop: simmer everything gently, uncovered, until just tender, keeping about an inch of water over the beans; drain and save the liquid. Canned: drain and rinse, and have 1 cup veggie broth ready.)
2. While the beans cook, make the gremolata, cornbread, and collards.
3. **Collards:** Trim the big stems and slice them thin; chop the leaves (shred or 1-inch pieces). Heat the oil in an extra-large skillet over medium. Add the garlic, chili flakes, and salt and stir until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add the stems and sauté 3 to 4 minutes, then add the leaves and gently wilt. For tender collards, add a splash of broth and cover a few minutes; for vibrant ones, keep them lightly cooked. Set aside.
4. **Smoky beans:** Wipe out the skillet and add the olive oil, onion, and garlic. Sauté until fragrant. Stir in the smoked paprika and allspice. Add the beans, ½ to 1 cup bean liquid (or broth), and salt. Keep them a little loose. Adjust seasoning — add cayenne for heat; if bland, they need salt.
5. **Assemble:** Scoop the black-eyed peas into a bowl, top with a hearty portion of greens, and dot with gremolata. Serve with cornbread.
