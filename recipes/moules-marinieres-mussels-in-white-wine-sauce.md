## Ingredients

- 2 lb mussels
- Kosher salt (for soaking + seasoning)
- 4 tbsp unsalted butter
- 1 shallot, finely chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, finely chopped
- 1 cup white wine (such as Sauvignon Blanc)
- 1 bay leaf
- 4–5 parsley sprigs (+ more leaves for garnish)
- 4–5 thyme sprigs
- 1/4 tsp ground black pepper

## Instructions

1. **Clean the mussels:** Scrub each mussel with a brush and pull off the beard. Place in a large bowl of cold water sprinkled with kosher salt, toss gently, and soak 20 minutes to release sand.
2. **Rinse and drain:** Lift the mussels out of the soaking water by hand into a colander (don't pour them in — leaves the sand behind). Rinse well under cold running water.
3. **Start the sauce:** In a large pot over medium heat, combine butter, shallot, garlic, wine, bay leaf, parsley, thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring to a good simmer and let the liquid reduce slightly, 2–3 minutes.
4. **Steam:** Add the mussels, cover, and cook until they just open, 5–7 minutes. Discard any that stay shut.
5. **Serve:** Transfer mussels to wide bowls, ladle the broth over, garnish with parsley, and serve with crusty bread.
