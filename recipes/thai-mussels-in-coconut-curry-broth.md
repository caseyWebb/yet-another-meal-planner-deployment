## Ingredients

- 1 teaspoon coconut oil
- 2 inch piece ginger (cut into matchsticks)
- 2 cloves garlic (sliced)
- 2 stalks lemongrass (sliced)
- 15 ounce can coconut milk (full-fat)
- 1 tablespoon fish sauce
- 2 Thai red chilis
- Juice from 1 lime
- 2 lb mussels (rinsed and beards removed)
- Thai basil (to serve)
- Jasmine rice or toasted baguette, to serve

## Instructions

1. Heat the coconut oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add the ginger, garlic, and lemongrass and cook for 1 minute. Add the coconut milk, fish sauce, chilis, and lime juice and bring the pot to a boil.
2. When the coconut milk is boiling, add the mussels. Cover the pot for 1 minute. After a minute, remove the lid and take out any shells that have opened. Continue to remove the mussels as they open. If any of the mussels are still closed after 6 or 7 minutes, discard them.
3. Pour the sauce over the cooked mussels, top with some Thai basil, and serve right away.
