## Ingredients

- 2 mackerel fillets (about 10 oz / 280g total), cut in half crosswise
- 2 tablespoons sake (non-alcoholic sub: water — the sake here mainly deodorizes and is patted off before cooking)
- ½ teaspoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt (or ¼ tsp table salt)
- 1-inch piece daikon radish (about 75g), peeled and grated
- 1 lemon wedge, halved
- 1 teaspoon soy sauce (for the daikon condiment)

## Instructions

1. Prep fish: Cut fillets in half crosswise if not already done. Coat all surfaces with sake.
2. Pat dry and salt: Pat the fish completely dry with paper towels, discarding the sake. Transfer to a parchment-lined baking sheet. Sprinkle both sides evenly with kosher salt.
3. Rest: Let sit at room temperature for 20 minutes. During this time, preheat oven to 425°F (218°C), rack in the middle position. For convection, reduce temperature by 25°F.
4. Pat dry again: After resting, pat the fish dry once more to remove the moisture drawn out by the salt.
5. Bake: Place fish skin-side down on the parchment. Bake 15–20 minutes until the flesh is cooked through and the top is golden.
6. Prep daikon: Grate the daikon and squeeze out most of the liquid.
7. Serve: Plate the mackerel with a mound of grated daikon and a lemon wedge. Drizzle a few drops of soy sauce over the daikon and use as a refreshing condiment alongside the fish.
