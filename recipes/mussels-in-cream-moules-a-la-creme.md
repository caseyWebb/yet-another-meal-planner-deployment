## Ingredients

- 1 lb mussels
- 1 shallot (or approx 1/4 small sweet onion)
- 1 clove garlic, small
- 1/2 tbsp butter (approximately)
- 3 tbsp white wine
- 1 stem thyme (fresh, or two if small)
- 1 bay leaf
- 1/4 cup creme fraiche (or a little more to taste)
- 1 stem parsley (approximately)
- Crusty bread, to serve

## Instructions

1. Rinse the mussels well in cold water, taking off any dirt, barnacles and hairy bits. Discard any that are broken, or open and don't close when you press them.
2. Finely dice the shallot/onion and garlic. Warm the butter in a pot/pan big enough to allow the mussels to expand over medium-low heat. Add the shallot, cook a minute or two to soften, then add the garlic. Stir and cook for a minute until aromatic. Add the wine, then the mussels, thyme and bay leaf. Stir to spread out the mussels, cover with a lid, and let the mussels steam. They should take only a couple of minutes to open. Give the pan a gentle shake if needed.
3. Once all of the mussels have opened, remove the pan from the heat. Add the creme fraiche and parsley and stir to mix into the wine-shallot sauce. Transfer the mussels and sauce to a serving bowl and serve immediately, ideally with crusty bread to mop up the sauce.
