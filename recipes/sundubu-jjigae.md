## Ingredients

**Broth:**
- 3 to 4 small dried shiitake mushrooms
- 0.5 oz dried kombu (kelp)
- 4 cups water

**Stew:**
- 1.5 tablespoons neutral cooking oil
- 1 small white onion, diced
- ½ cup scallions, chopped (white and green parts separated)
- 2 to 3 tablespoons gochugaru (Korean chili powder)
- ¾ cup chopped vegan kimchi
- 1 tablespoon rice wine (mirin/sake), optional
- 2 to 3 tablespoons soy sauce (or tamari), to taste
- 1 bundle (150 g) fresh enoki mushrooms
- 1 lb sliced fresh king oyster or shiitake mushrooms
- 1 medium zucchini, sliced into ¼-inch quarters
- 3 cups mushroom-kombu broth (from above) or vegetable broth
- 1 teaspoon minced garlic
- ½ teaspoon salt, or more to taste
- Pinch sugar
- 1 lb sundubu (soft/silken tofu)
- Green scallion parts, for garnish

## Instructions

1. **Broth:** Soak the kombu and dried shiitake in the water overnight in the fridge for best flavor (yields about 3 cups). Quick method: boil them 10 to 15 minutes, then steep 10 to 15 minutes. Remove the kombu and mushrooms (slice the rehydrated mushrooms to add back if you like).
2. Heat a medium pot or ttukbaegi over medium and add the oil. Sauté the onion and scallion whites 2 to 3 minutes until translucent.
3. Add the gochugaru and cook 1 to 2 minutes to bloom it into a chili oil.
4. Add the kimchi and sauté 2 to 3 minutes. Add the soy sauce (and rice wine, if using) over high heat until it slightly reduces.
5. Add the king oyster/shiitake mushrooms and some enoki; sauté about 3 minutes.
6. Add the zucchini and cook 2 to 3 minutes.
7. Pour in the broth, cover, and boil over medium-high about 7 minutes, until the zucchini and mushrooms are cooked. Season with salt and a pinch of sugar.
8. Stir in the garlic. Add the soft tofu and remaining enoki on top and boil 2 to 3 minutes more, breaking the tofu into the stew.
9. Garnish with scallion greens. Serve hot with steamed short-grain rice and kimchi.
