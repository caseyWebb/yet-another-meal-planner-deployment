## Ingredients

- 500 g spaghetti or bucatini (1.1 lb)
- 125 g guanciale (4.4 oz) — keep the lean, fatty, and peppered outer parts
- 80 g finely grated Pecorino Romano DOP (⅔ cup)
- 400 g canned San Marzano (or other good whole peeled) tomatoes (about 2 cups)
- 1 fresh or dried chili pepper
- Salt, to taste

> **No-alcohol note:** Traditional amatriciana sometimes uses a splash (~¼ cup) of dry white wine to deglaze, but it's optional here and the dish is fully authentic without it. Skip it; if you want the extra acidity it would add, finish with a small squeeze of lemon or a splash of pasta water instead.

## Instructions

1. Cut the guanciale into thin strips, keeping the lean, fatty, and dark peppered outer parts.
2. Add the chili and guanciale to a cold pan over medium heat — no oil needed; the guanciale renders its own fat.
3. Sauté, stirring, until the fat is translucent and golden, about 5 minutes.
4. Add the canned tomatoes with their juice. Crush gently with a fork into a pulpy consistency. Season with a small pinch of salt (Pecorino is salty — go light).
5. Simmer over medium heat about 10 minutes, until slightly thickened but still juicy. Remove and discard the chili.
6. Meanwhile, cook the pasta al dente in salted water, reserving a cup of pasta water before draining.
7. Transfer the drained pasta to the sauce and toss over low heat to coat.
8. Sprinkle in the Pecorino, stirring quickly to melt and bind. Loosen with pasta water if too thick. Serve immediately with extra Pecorino.
