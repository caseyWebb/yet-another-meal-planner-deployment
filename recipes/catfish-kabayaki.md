A budget-friendly take on unagi donburi: basa (catfish) fillets pan-fried until golden and glazed in a sweet-savory kabayaki sauce. Ready in 15 minutes.

## Ingredients

- 2 basa fillets (catfish fillets)
- 1½ Tbsp all-purpose flour
- ⅛ tsp kosher salt
- ⅛ tsp black pepper
- 1½ Tbsp neutral oil
- 3 Tbsp soy sauce
- 2 Tbsp mirin
- 2 Tbsp sake
- 1 Tbsp sugar
- 2 servings cooked Japanese short-grain rice
- 2 green onions/scallions
- 1 tsp toasted white sesame seeds
- Japanese sansho pepper (optional)

## Instructions

1. Cut basa fillets crosswise in half. Season both sides with salt and pepper. Lightly coat with flour and shake off excess.
2. Heat oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Fry fillets 3–4 minutes per side until golden brown and flesh is opaque.
3. In a small bowl, whisk soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar until sugar dissolves. Pour over fish and cook 1–2 minutes, spooning over the fish, until sauce thickens and coats the back of a spoon.
4. Thinly slice scallions and sprinkle over fish. Turn off heat.
5. Divide rice into donburi bowls, top with fish, drizzle with pan sauce, and sprinkle with sesame seeds and sansho pepper.
